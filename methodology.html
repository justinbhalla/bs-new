At-a-glance

Units & currency
We label units; currency is INR unless stated. USD conversions use daily RBI reference or exchange quotes as noted.
Deflators & bases
Real series use CPI/WPI/GDP deflators. Index bases (100 = base-date) are specified in captions.
Smoothing
We prefer trailing averages (3/6/12-M) with raw series also shown or available.

Charting rules

  • Axes Axes and units are explicit; left axis for primary measure, right axis used sparingly.
  • Scales Linear by default; log scale when growth ratios matter or spans are wide (clearly labeled).
  • Ranges Zero baseline for quantities; trimmed ranges allowed when emphasizing variation (annotated).
  • Colors High contrast, color-blind friendly. One series = one hue; uncertainty bands use opacity.
  • Labels Titles state metric, geography, and period; subtitles mention deflators or adjustments.
  • Data gaps Missing values are not interpolated unless stated; gaps are visually indicated.

Rounding & figures

  • Percentages: 1 decimal unless tiny samples.
  • Large rupee amounts use crore/lakh formatting or SI (B/M) depending on context.
  • Index values show 0 decimals; rates show 2 decimals.

Composite indices

  • Inputs are standardized (z-scores) over a rolling window unless level units are comparable.
  • Weights are equal unless stated; expert or regression weights are disclosed.
  • Outliers beyond ±4σ are winsorized or annotated with rationale.
  • Backfilled history uses consistent methodology; breaks are flagged.

Publishing

We publish definitions, constituents, and weight tables with each composite dashboard.

Uncertainty & error

  • Survey-based estimates show confidence intervals where available.
  • Model estimates are labeled “Estimate”; bands represent model/past error, not guarantees.
  • We avoid false precision; round to meaningful granularity.

Revisions policy

  • When sources revise history, we refresh affected charts promptly and annotate material effects.
  • Base-year or methodology changes are noted in captions and, for major series, in a brief explainer.
  • We archive prior outputs when comparisons matter (e.g., GDP series across base changes).

Change log

Major methodology changes appear on the relevant dashboard/tool page’s changelog.

Backtests & performance

  • Backtests are hypothetical and reflect assumptions shown on the page.
  • We use realistic costs/slippage where relevant, and state survivorship/data snooping caveats.
  • Benchmarks and windows are pre-declared; no cherry-picking of favorable periods.

Citations & provenance

  • Each chart lists “Source(s)” with links plus “bataSutra calculations” when derived.
  • Method notes appear in a tooltip/footnote; full details link to this Methodology page.
  • See Data Sources for our source catalog and licensing notes.
